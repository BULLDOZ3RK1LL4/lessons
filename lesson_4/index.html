Santa-Monica

Santa Monica - is a city in the United States , located in the west of Los Angeles County, California . The city is named after Saint Monica , a Christian saint and mother of Aurelius Augustine . The name owes its origin to the Spaniards visiting this place during a Christian holiday. It is bordered by the Pacific Ocean Bay of Santa Monica to the west and the following areas of Los Angeles: Pacific Palisades and Brentwood to the north, West Los Angeles and Mar Vista to the east, and Venice to the south. Thus, with the exception of the Pacific Ocean to the west, Santa Monica is completely surrounded by Los Angeles . According to the results of the 2010 census, the population is 89,736 people.

History

Santa Monica was part of the Tongwa American Indian Territory. Santa Monica has been known since 1769 , in which the Spanish pioneer Gaspar de Portola landed in the area of ​​modern Wilshire Boulevard.

Geography

The geographic coordinates of Santa Monica are 34°1'19" N, 118°28'53" W. e. According to the United States Census Bureau, the total area of ​​Santa Monica is 41.2 km², including 21.4 km² land and 19.8 km² water (48.08%). Height above sea level - 32 m.

Climate

The city enjoys a subtropical Mediterranean climate, like most cities along the coast of southern California, which results in the region experiencing mild, fairly wet winters and warm, even hot, often dry summers. The number of sunny days in a year is 325. Due to Santa Monica's geographic location, morning fog is common in May, June and early July. June is often cloudy and cool, while Los Angeles can be warm and sunny. The daily temperature in spring (April) is +20 °C. In summer, the temperature on the coast rises to +30 °C. The rainy season (however, quite rare) can last from late October to mid-March. In winter, the average maximum temperature during the day is +17 °C. Pacific breezes make Santa Monica's air fresher and cleaner than inland Los Angeles.

Population

According to the 2000 census, there were 84,084 residents, 44,497 households and 16,775 families in the city. The population density was thus 3,930.4 persons per km². The racial makeup of the city was 78.29% White, 3.78% African American, 0.47% Indian, 7.25% Asian, 0.10% Pacific Islander , 5.97% Other. races. 13.44% of the population identified their origin as Hispanic (Hispanic). Of the 44,497 households at the census date, 15.8% had children, 27.5% were married couples, 7.5% were headed by single mothers, and 62.3% were non-families. 51.2% of households were formed by single people. The average number of people in a household was 1.83. Age composition of the population: 14.6% under 18 years old, 6.1% from 18 to 24 years old, 40.1% from 25 to 44 years old, 24.8% from 45 to 64 years old and 14.4% - 65 years old and older. The average age is 39 years. For every 100 women, there are 93.0 men. The median annual income for a man in 2000 was $55,689; for a woman, $42,948.

Transport

The I-10 transcontinental highway originates in Santa Monica near the ocean and heads east. Santa Monica has its own bus company, Big Blue Bus [en] (Big Blue Bus), which also serves West Los Angeles, the Pacific Palisades, Venice , and the UCLA area . Santa Monica has its own airport - Santa Monica Airport.

Economy

Several large businesses are located in Santa Monica: video game companies Activision and Naughty Dog, subsidiary LowerMyBill.com, Experian, Business.com, affiliates of Apple , Microsoft , Yahoo , Google , Universal Music Group , Hulu , and MTV . Former companies include Douglas Aircraft, taken over by Boeing, and MySpace (now located in Beverly Hills )

Culture

There are several museums in Santa Monica: the Heritage Museum (Santa Monica Heritage Museum), the Museum of Art (Santa Monica Museum of Art). From 1961 to 1968, the city's concert hall (Santa Monica Civic Auditorium) hosted the Academy Awards. Santa Monica's oldest movie theater, the Majestic, closed after the 1994 earthquake. At the end of October 2010, five cinemas were operating in Santa Monica , two of which (Aero Theater and Criterion Theater) were built back in the 30s. More British and Irish people live in Santa Monica than in all of Los Angeles , so there are many British and Irish pubs in the city.
